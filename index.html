Unfamiliar Text Techniques

Hover (or tap, on mobile) on a card to flip it and show the answer.


Simile
A comparison between two things using "like" or "as".
Metaphor
A comparison that makes a comparison with "is".
Extended Metaphor
An overall theme for effect, without stating it ("they attacked our defences" implies a theme of battle).
Personification
Giving human qualities to an inanimate object.
Hyperbole
Exaggerating something for effect ("there were millions of people")
Pun
A play on words, usually with the intent of a humorous effect.
Neologism
A newly made-up word, usually for a specific purpose.
Imperative
A command that does not allow for uncertainty ("get it now!", "go to your room!").
Synonym
Different words with same or similar meaning.
Antonym
Different words with opposite meanings.
Verbs
"Doing words" - running, reading, cooking, sleeping.
Proper Nouns
Place, person or event names. Always capitalised.
Abstract Nouns
Nouns that cannot be quantified - freedom, love, destiny.
Oxymoron
Two images together which contradict each other, but to create an effect ("the fire chilled him to the bone").
Paradox
A statement which contradicts itself ("this statement is false").
Generalizations
Making a wide appeal for emotions, avoiding the exceptions that might confuse intention.
Connotations
"Loaded words" with strong positive or negative meaning.
Euphemism
A polite way of saying something - "passed away" vs "died".
Analogy
An interesting or poetic connection between two things (often also a metaphor).
Allusion
Referencing (indirectly or directly) another text.
Rhetorical Question
Asking a question without expecting a reply - normally to make the reader think about a problem.
Listing
A list - "yes, no, maybe".
Cliche
A phrase that (through overuse) has lost it's meaning or effect - "sparkled like the sun". Can be ironic.
Comparative
One thing is "better, faster, stronger" than the other.
Superlative
One thing is the "best, fastest, strongest" than the other.
Acronym
Shortening a phrase to it's initials - EFTPOS = Electronic Funds Transfer at Point Of Sale.
Contraction
Shortening two words to one with an apostrophe (making the tone less formal) - do not > don't, can not > can't.
Antithesis
Complete opposite. Usually used as a dramatic comparison.
Jargon
Language that is specific to a field or profession.
Alliteration
Two or more words consecutively (or nearly consecutively) with the same starting letter or sound - "Alice ate all the apples".
Assonance
Similar to alliteration - repetition of vowel sounds within words (2 or more) - "brown cow".
Consonance
Assonance, but with consonant sounds.
Sibilance
Alliteration but only with the letter S.
Onomatopoeia
Words that describe a sound literally - "bang", "crash", "boom".
Symbolism
An image which represents an idea - e.g. heart > love, lightning bolt > electricity.
Motif
Repeated image, idea or symbol.
Repetition
Repeating a word, phrase or idea to emphasise it.
Imagery
Using figurative language to vividly describe a scene or action, usually so the reader can picture it in their mind.
Anaphora
Repeating a word or phrase at the beginning of clauses - "it was the best, it was the worst". Applies almost exclusively to poetry or song lyrics.
Refrain
Repeating a key sentence or phrase, when used in poems and song lyrics.
