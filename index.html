Unfamiliar Text Techniques

Hover (or tap, on mobile) on a card to flip it and show the answer.


Adjective A word that describes a noun or another adjective.

'The air was full of driving, needle- pointed icicles'
Adverbs A word that adds information about a verb. An adverb tells how, why, when, or where. They often end in '-ly'.

'Slowly, stealthily, the wind was lifting a swell'
Alliteration Repetition of the same consonant (non- vowel) sound at the beginning of words that are close together.

'Having heard the song, he sang it softly'
Allusion An indirect reference to a person, event, thing or to a part of another text.

'I didn't have any bus fare, but fortunately some good Samaritan helped me out!'
Assonance Vowel sounds are repeated at the beginning or middle of words that are close together.
Connotations A feeling or emotion from hearing a specific word or phrase.

Positive: 'Kevin is very determined'
Negative: 'Kevin is very stubborn'
Contrasts Using words of opposite meaning close together

'The sun warms on a bitterly cold day'
Counter-argument Criticism of the opposite opinion.
Emotive Language Choice of words intended to evoke an emotional response.

Non-Emotive: I saw a video about whaling
Emotive: I saw a video about the brutal slaughter of whales.
First Person Pronouns Words that refer to the speaker (I, we, us, me).

'I opened the door wide, then opened my eyes wider when I saw a small black dog sitting on the step.'
Hyperbole Deliberate exaggeration for effect.

'I've told you thousands of times to clean your room.
Imagery Thought-provoking pictures in your mind. Helps the reader to 'see' the scenario.
Jargon Vocabulary peculiar to a particular trade, profession, or group.
Juxtaposition Two things being seen or placed close together with contrasting effect.

'It was the best of times, it was the worst of times, it was the age of wisdom, it was the age of foolishness...'
Listing A series of objects / reasons etc., usually with commas separating them.
Metaphor Describes something as being something else (without using the words 'like' or 'as').

'The playground was a ghost town.'
Repetition A word, phrase or sentence structure is deliberately repeated for effect.

'Come on, come on!' she shouted. 'We're late!'
Rhyme The ends of words have the same sound. Usually at the ends of lines in poetry, but may be internal (within a line).
Onomatopoeia Words which describe sounds and also sound like what they are describing.

'Plop', 'whisper', 'rustle', 'whoosh'
Personification When non-human objects are given human characteristics.

'The wind moaned.'
Quotation Direct use of another's words (spoken or written).

'Winston Churchill famously said, "We will fight them on the beaches...."'
Rhetorical Question A question that is asked without an answer being expected. It might also be one that has an obvious answer but the question has been asked to make a point.
Second Person Using words such as you, your, yours
Short Sentences Short sentence or one sentence paragraphs.
Metaphor Describes something as being something else (using the words 'like' or 'as').

‘The playground was as empty as a ghost town.’
Statistics A specific, detailed number or fact.
Symbol A sign (or thing) representing something.

e.g. the silver fern, which is a symbol of New Zealand.
